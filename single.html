How does cleaning our oceans provide peace in our world?

Many rallies, events and fundraisers that promote peace usually are against physical and violent issues. Most people are unaware of the most or one of the most pressing issues that if not solved could cause the opposite of peace, destruction. Like climate change itself, many people brush off the topic of keeping our oceans clean because they are unaware of the harmful consequences. Studies have shown that oceans generate half of the oxygen we breathe. The ocean also contains more than 97% of the world's water. Additionally, oceans provide at least a sixth of the animal protein people eat. Taking care of our earth’s oceans also matters economically. The ocean is the way goods and other supplies are transported. In 2016, the U.S. maritime transportation system carried $1.5 trillion of cargo through U.S. seaports to and from our international trading partners. So, the U.S. economy is very dependent on healthy coastal and ocean resources.

When it comes to energy, energy sources such as wave power, tides and ocean currents are almost 100 percent predictable. Moving water, ocean waves is 832 times denser than air in our atmosphere, which multiplies the kinetic energy of ocean waves by the same factor. Ocean currents are created by regional differences in temperature, salinity and the Coriolis effect due to the rotation of the earth. Ocean currents flow continuously in the same direction with low variability. This means ocean current energy represents a completely reliable non-intermittent energy source. Despite this, 88% of our oceans is filled with plastic debris.



The dictionary definition of peace is the freedom from disturbance, tranquility. Our oceans help provide peace to our world from preventing disturbance that can cause harm to anyone and everything on this planet. Ocean currents regulate global climate by counteracting the uneven distribution of solar radiation reaching Earth's surface. The ocean transports heat from the equator to the poles which regulates our climate and weather patterns. The ocean does not only store solar radiation and heat. Oceans also help distribute heat all around the globe. Once, water molecules are heated, they exchange freely with the air through evaporation. Ocean water is also constantly evaporating, increasing the temperature and humidity of the surrounding air to form rain and storms that are then carried by trade winds. In fact, almost all rain that falls on land starts off in the ocean. Kendall Jones, who works at the University of Queensland, Australia, and who is a part of the Wildlife Conservation Society, states that, “The ocean is immense, covering over 70% of our planet, but we’ve managed to significantly impact almost all of this vast ecosystem, just 13% of the world’s oceans remain untouched by humanity.”

By allowing plastic to fill up our oceans, this issue now involves our health. As Styrofoam breaks into smaller parts, polystyrene components in it sink lower in the ocean, so that the pollutant spreads throughout the sea column. In fact, toxins in plastic affect the ocean act like sponges, they soak up other toxins from outside sources before entering the ocean. As these chemicals are ingested by animals in the ocean, this is not good for humans. So this means that we as humans are ingesting contaminated fish and mammals.
