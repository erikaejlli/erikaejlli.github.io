In Yonkers, there has been debate about Hillview Reservoir. New York City has agreed to cover the Reservoir, in Yonkers, to protect the water supply that serves the nation's most populous city, which the U.S. Attorney's office claims.
The eastern basin of the 90-acre reservoir is expected to be covered in 2042 and the western basin in 2049. But the court agreement between New York City and the federal government, which still requires a judge's approval, allows the possibility for the cover could be finished sooner.
The cost estimate is not immediately known. But an estimate in 2009, for what was then a planned concrete cover for the reservoir, was $1.6 billion. The U.S. Attorney's office said in a statement that the price tag may be lower if the city chooses a different kind of cover. Monday's agreement settles a complaint the federal government bought against New York City and the city's Department of Environmental Protection for allegedly violating regulation and orders for years in dealing with covering the reservoir.



Hillview Reservoir is an open holding tank that is the final point before drinking water enters New York City tunnels to residents.
Water undergoes chlorine and ultraviolet treatment before it enters Hillview Reservoir. The reservoir is downstream of those treatments and is an open facility, so the U.S. Attorney's office said the water "is subject to recontamination with pathogens, such as viruses from birds and other animals and sources and contamination from ocean pollution. Until it's covered, New York City takes measures to control wildlife in and around the reservoir and monitor the water.
