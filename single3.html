Peacemaking Strategy

As far as how teenagers can help rid of our oceans filth, we can not grab all of the filth out of the ocean with our bare hands. One thing that all teenagers and adults can do is support Organizations Addressing Plastic Pollution. By donating to organizations like Oceana, The Surfrider Organization. According to a report done by scientists who are a part of Oceana, a wastage equivalent of a truckload enters into our oceans every minute, every day, all year long. Oceana in particular group focuses on educating the public and also advocating for policy changes for maintaining ocean wildlife. By joining organizations, we can be a part of others peacemaking strategies.



List of Teen Action Ideas:

  • Pick up trash that gets deposited in the ocean itself and its surroundings;
  • Reduce the use of plastics;
  • Set up beach and river cleanups;
  • Reduce energy consumption;
  • Avoid using products containing microbeads that may contaminate the ocean;
  • Properly dispose trash;
  • Reduce the amount of carbon dioxide used by;
  • Ride a bike, walk, or use public transportation instead of a car;
  • Turn off the lights as you leave a room;
  • Instead of turning up the thermostat in the winter wear something cozy and warm;
  • Eat sustainable seafood to not affect the wildlife in the oceans;
  • Voting on issues that can help protect the ocean as well as making this issue known to representatives so they can help reduce filth found in the ocean;
  • Spreading the word to friends and peers about the issues and stating how they can help. Setting up a program for you and your peers to clean the surroundings of the ocean, so no unwanted residue would go there.
